Our Story in a Nutshell

BioGreen is a biotechnology student-led sustainability initiative at the University of Nottingham that began with a simple question — can waste from one industry help another grow greener? Every year, breweries produce tonnes of spent grain, a nutrient-rich by-product that usually ends up discarded. We decided to give it a second life.

By using this material as a natural fertiliser, our team of students has been exploring how waste can fuel plant growth and reduce the need for chemical fertilisers. What started as a small volunteer experiment has evolved into a collaborative project that blends science, sustainability, and community action. With support from Dr Nagamani Bora and Neon Raptor Brewing Co., we've turned campus allotments into living research plots — learning, testing, and growing together.

BioGreen isn't just about data; it's about people. It's about building a community of students, gardeners, and local partners who share a belief that small changes can make real environmental impact.
